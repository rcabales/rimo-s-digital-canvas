# Why Your Business Needs a Custom Website (Not Just a Template)

*Or: why your online presence shouldn't look like everyone else's rental property*

---

Let's start with a little thought experiment. Imagine you're opening a bakery. You've spent months perfecting your sourdough, sourcing local ingredients, and coming up with a name that perfectly captures your vibe. You're ready to tell the world.

Now imagine you show up to your grand opening… and so does every other bakery in town. Same signage. Same layout. Same font on the menu. Same little bell above the door.

That's essentially what happens when you build your business website on a generic template.

---

## "But Templates Are So Easy!"

Yes, they are. And honestly? For some situations, they're completely fine. If you're testing an idea, just getting started, or running a very simple operation, a template can get you online quickly and cheaply. No argument there.

But here's the thing — a template is designed to work for *everyone*, which means it's optimised for *no one*. It's a one-size-fits-all solution, and as anyone who's ever bought one-size-fits-all socks knows… they don't really fit all.

---

## What a Custom Website Actually Means

A custom website is built specifically for *your* business, *your* customers, and *your* goals. It's not just about looking pretty (though it will). It's about function.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

- **Your booking system actually works the way your business works.** Not the way a template from 2019 assumed businesses work.
- **Your website loads fast.** Templates often come bloated with code for features you'll never use — like carrying around a toolkit when all you needed was a screwdriver.
- **It grows with you.** Need to add an online shop, a client portal, or a membership area later? A custom build can accommodate that without you having to start from scratch.
- **It actually shows up on Google.** Custom sites can be built with search engine optimisation (SEO) baked in from the start, rather than bolted on as an afterthought.

---

## The "But My Cousin Made Me a Website" Problem

We've all been there. Someone in the family knows "a bit about computers," and suddenly you have a website that… exists. It has your logo on it (slightly stretched). There's a contact form (that may or may not send emails). And it looks fine on a desktop, but on a phone it resembles a Picasso painting.

This isn't a dig at your cousin — it's just that building a website that genuinely works for a business takes more than enthusiasm and a free weekend.

A professional developer builds something that works properly, looks intentional, and won't quietly break every time someone visits it on a Samsung.

---

## "Okay, But Isn't It Really Expensive?"

It can be more of an upfront investment than a template, yes. But consider what your website is actually doing (or *should* be doing): bringing in customers, explaining what you offer, building trust, and converting curious visitors into paying clients. 

A website that does all of that well? That pays for itself. A website that confuses people or looks untrustworthy? That quietly costs you business every single day — you just never see it happening.

---

## The Bottom Line

Your website is often the first impression someone has of your business. Before they call you, email you, or walk through your door — they've Googled you. What they find will either make them confident in reaching out, or send them quietly clicking over to your competitor.

A custom website says: *we take our business seriously, and we'll take your needs seriously too.*

A template says: *we found something that was good enough.*

Which one sounds more like you?
