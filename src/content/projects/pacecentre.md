## The Brief

The Pace Centre is a UK charity and specialist school supporting children aged 0-18 with neurodisabilities including cerebral palsy, established in 1990. The project was a complete redesign and rebuild, elevating both their online presence and brand credibility for an organisation that depends on public trust and funding.

![Pace Centre screenshot](/projects/pacecentre/pacecentre1.png)

## My Role

I led the project end to end, covering both development and project management. That dual responsibility is a different challenge to pure development work. You're making scope and priority calls that affect the client relationship while simultaneously making technical decisions that affect the build. Getting those two roles confused is how projects go wrong.

![Pace Centre screenshot](/projects/pacecentre/pacecentre2.png)

## How I Structured the Build

I brought in a second developer for a defined set of complex features, a deliberate decision to protect the overall project timeline rather than attempt everything myself. Keeping the scope of that contribution tightly defined meant I could maintain momentum on the broader build without creating coordination overhead.

The nature of the client, a charity working with vulnerable children, made accessibility non-negotiable rather than aspirational. That shaped technical decisions throughout the build.

## What I'd Do Differently

Running development and project management simultaneously stretches attention in ways that eventually cost you somewhere. On a project of this scope I'd push for a clearer separation between those roles earlier, even if that means a more explicit conversation with the client about how the project is being run. The alternative is making compromises you regret later.
