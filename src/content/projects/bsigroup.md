## The client

The British Standards Institution (BSI) is the UK's national standards body, established in 1901. BSI offers a comprehensive range of services to help organizations improve performance, manage risk, and achieve sustainable growth. These services include standards development, management systems assessment and certification, product testing and certification (such as the BSI Kitemark), training programs, and consulting services.

![BSI screenshot](/projects/bsi/bsi1.png)

BSI operates globally, serving clients in 195 countries, with a workforce of over 5,200 employees as of 2020. The organization focuses on key areas such as digital trust, health, safety and well-being, innovation, quality, supply chain management, and sustainability, aiming to empower organizations to inspire trust and drive excellence.

## The project + what did I do?

This project involved a complete redesign and rebuild of the site, as requested by the client. I was part of a large development team, working as one of two front-end specialists. Our primary responsibility was to build a wide range of front-end components, which were then handed over to the back-end team for integration with their bespoke CMS.

![BSI screenshot](/projects/bsi/bsi2.png)

We collaborated closely with the design team, providing guidance and recommendations on accessibility and user experience best practices.

To ensure the build remained lean and efficient, we relied on vanilla JavaScript complemented by a few carefully selected libraries.
